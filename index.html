RESEARCH

UNIVERSITY OF VIRGINIA, UVA

Project Description: My doctoral research has evolved from an early focus on microglial P2RY12 signaling in homeostatic brain function to a deeper exploration of its transcriptional and metabolic roles in disease. Initially, I investigated how P2RY12 mediates microglia–astrocyte interactions important for vascular homeostasis and neurovascular coupling.

Building on that foundation, my dissertation now centers on the transcriptional regulation of microglial activation states and metabolism by P2RY12, highlighted in my recent first-author publication in Glia (2025). This work demonstrates that P2RY12 loss alters metabolic and antioxidant pathways, including glutathione-GPX4–related ferroptosis susceptibility, especially during inflammatory stress.

Currently, I am extending these findings into the disease context, to assess how P2RY12 deficiency shapes microglial responses to pathology and neurodegeneration.

Sponsor: Ukpong Eyo Laboratory, BIG Center, Neuroscience Graduate Program, University of Virginia, Charlottesville, VA, USA. (Jul 2021 - Present)
