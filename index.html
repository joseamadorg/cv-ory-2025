PHD STUDENT / UNIVERSITY OF VIRGINIA, UVA / 2026

The University of Virginia (UVA) was founded in 1819 by Thomas Jefferson and is located in Charlottesville, Virginia. UVA is consistently ranked among the top public universities in the United States and is well-known for its commitment to academic excellence and research.

The Neuroscience Program at UVA is a highly respected interdisciplinary program that combines the study of biology, psychology, and computer science to understand the workings of the human brain. The program provides students with a comprehensive education in neuroscience, including coursework in molecular, cellular, and systems neuroscience, as well as research opportunities in cutting-edge areas such as neurodegenerative diseases, neural development, and the neural basis of behavior and cognition.

UVA's Neuroscience Program has a long history of producing graduates who go on to make significant contributions to the field of neuroscience, both in academia and industry. The program's faculty members are renowned experts in their respective areas of research, and are dedicated to providing students with a rigorous and engaging educational experience.
